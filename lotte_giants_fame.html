Fame

Lotte is South Korea's most popular club recognized by oneself and others. Lotte became the first club to surpass the 1 million mark in 1991. Since then, it had recorded 1 million attendance in 1992, 1995, 2008, and 2009. In particular, Lotte proved itself to be the most popular team in 2009 with a record attendance of 1,380,018 people.

In a survey conducted by Gallup Korea in 2011, the Lotte Giants were also named the most popular club. Based on TV ratings, the Lotte Giants are again enjoying high popularity. According to the 2008 season ratings compiled by ratings agency TNmS and AGB Nielsen Media Research Korea top 10 games with the highest ratings were all Lotte Giants games in 2007 and 2008.

Lotte's club has the highest value in the league as of 2010. According to a survey conducted by Chebul.com, Lotte Giants ranked first among professional baseball teams, valued at 350.9 billion won.

Its unique cheering culture plays a major role in its such fame. The audience voluntarily developed a cheering tool made up of newspapers and shake them. When it enters the latter part of the game, they wear an orange plastic bag distributed by the club on his head, which can be used for garbage collection after the game ends. One of its signiture cheering songs are "Busan Seagull" and "Come back To Busan Harbor"
